# Sudheer's Tone Guide
# Sources: Carlson interview prep, MBA essays, conversation style, JobFlow OS session
# Last updated: March 2026

## How I sound
Register: Direct and technical-casual — confident, not arrogant
Energy: A builder talking to builders or the people who lead them
Vocabulary: Specific, active, plain — technical when it earns its place

In one sentence:
I say exactly what I mean, I name the real thing I built, and I cut everything in between.

## What I NEVER say — banned phrases, zero exceptions
- I hope this message finds you well
- I hope this finds you well
- I'd love to / I would love to
- I came across your profile
- I was really impressed by
- I'm very passionate about
- Excited to connect
- I'd be honored
- Looking forward to hearing from you
- I noticed we share similar interests
- Exploring new opportunities
- Open to new challenges
- Results-driven / proven track record
- Leverage (as a business verb)
- Synergy / circle back / touch base
- Impactful / seasoned professional / dynamic
- Journey (when describing a career)
- I just wanted to reach out
- Seeking opportunities / I believe I would be a great fit

## How I open a message — priority order

1. Mutual connection → always lead with this if it exists
   "We're both connected to [Name]..."

2. Their recent LinkedIn activity → reference the exact topic
   "Your post on [specific topic] last week..."

3. Something specific from their profile — a role transition, a company, a project
   "Saw you moved from [Company A] to building [thing] at [Company B]..."

4. What I'm building + why it connects to what they do
   "Building multi-agent AI systems with Claude API — your infra work maps to exactly that."

Never: "I came across your profile and was impressed"

## How I close — short, no ask in the first message

Closings I use:
- "Worth connecting."
- "Wanted to be in your network."
- "Would value your perspective."
- "Thought it was worth connecting."

Closings I never use:
- "Looking forward to connecting with you!"
- "Would love to schedule a 30-minute call to explore opportunities."
- "Please feel free to reach out if you'd like to chat further."

## Message length — calibrated by seniority

VP / Director / C-Suite:       target 120-180 chars  (1-2 lines — they won't read more)
Senior Manager / Head of:      target 180-240 chars
Senior IC / Staff level:       target 200-260 chars
Manager / PM / Engineer:       target 200-280 chars
Hard ceiling: 300 characters (LinkedIn limit — never exceed)

## My strongest angles — use the right one per person

Engineering Managers:
  Lead with builder angle — I spent 4 years shipping Java enterprise systems
  at RNTBCI for Nissan, Renault, and Mitsubishi. I speak their language.

Heads of Product / Directors:
  Lead with AI/automation angle — I am building production multi-agent systems.
  That is the gap most product orgs have right now.

Senior ICs (Staff Engineers, Principal PMs):
  Peer energy. Name the stack or the problem specifically.
  Don't talk about outcomes — talk about the actual work.

AI-native companies:
  Just show the build. "Building multi-agent AI systems with Claude API..."
  No positioning needed — the work speaks.

Enterprise / automotive / consulting:
  Reference RNTBCI context — I have been inside that world.
  I know what legacy modernisation looks like from the inside.

## Tone by company type

AI-native:          Most casual. Builder-to-builder. Skip formality entirely.
Scale-stage:        Direct, slightly more structured. Reference their scale challenge.
Enterprise/public:  Still crisp — not stiff. Reference RNTBCI enterprise context.
Early-stage:        Most casual. Lead with the shared problem, not credentials.

Worth connecting."
