You have Sudheer's profile, enrichment data for this role, and learned preferences.
Write a positioning framework — not a cover letter, not a summary.
This is the single document every other agent reads to understand how to represent
Sudheer for this specific role.

ABOUT SUDHEER:
Full name: Sudheer Rayudu
Background: Full-stack engineer. 4 years at RNTBCI (Renault-Nissan tech centre, Chennai)
building enterprise Java systems — legacy modernisation, microservices, OKTA auth,

CRISP OVER COMPREHENSIVE. SHORT OVER THOROUGH.
If you are padding a section to seem thorough, you are doing it wrong.
A 10-word bullet that is specific beats a 3-sentence paragraph that is vague.

WRITE EXACTLY THESE SECTIONS — use these exact headers, no others:

HEADLINE:
  One sentence. Maximum 15 words.
  Must name what Sudheer IS — not what he wants or is looking for.
  Must reference something specific from THIS role or company — not generic.
  BAD: "Experienced engineer seeking product management opportunities"
  BAD: "Technical professional with diverse background looking for new challenges"
  GOOD: "Enterprise Java engineer turned AI builder — built for Stripe's infrastructure PM needs"
  GOOD: "4 years modernising legacy systems at scale — now building AI agents that do the same"

KEY_STRENGTHS:
  Exactly 5 bullets. Maximum 12 words each.
  Format for each: [what Sudheer specifically did] — [why it matters for THIS role]
  Each must connect a real thing from his profile to a specific signal from the enrichment.
  BAD: "Strong technical background with product awareness"
  GOOD: "Built 4 internal tools used daily at RNTBCI — maps to their internal tooling needs"
  GOOD: "Modernised legacy Java systems at automotive scale — exactly their current infra challenge"
  GOOD: "Active AI agent builder — directly relevant to their stated AI product roadmap"

TALKING_POINTS:
  4-6 bullets. Story-form sentences. Specific. Active voice. Present tense where possible.
  These are what the message agent pulls from when describing Sudheer.
  No buzzwords. No "passionate about". No "excited to". No hedging.
  BAD: "I am passionate about leveraging technology to drive business outcomes"
  GOOD: "I spent 4 years inside an automotive enterprise — I know what breaks at scale"
  GOOD: "I am building multi-agent AI systems in production right now, not in a course"
  GOOD: "I was promoted twice in 3 years by taking on legacy systems others avoided"

GAP_FRAMING:
  Look at role_signals from enrichment. Identify real gaps vs Sudheer's profile.
  Write 1-3 honest, confident sentences on how to frame each gap.
  If there are no meaningful gaps: write exactly "No significant gaps identified."
  Do not hide gaps. Do not pretend. Frame them as adjacent or transferable — honestly.
  BAD: "While I don't have X, I am a fast learner and pick things up quickly"
  GOOD: "Role asks for B2B SaaS PM experience — Sudheer's enterprise product ownership at
         RNTBCI is adjacent and the builder credibility closes most of that gap"

OUTREACH_TONE:
  Exactly 2 sentences. Tell the message agent exactly how to sound for this company.
  Derive from culture_signals in the enrichment — not from guessing.
  BAD: "Use a professional and friendly tone"
  GOOD: "This company values directness and builder culture — lead with what Sudheer built,
         not what he studied. Keep it short, confident, zero corporate language."

BANNED WORDS AND PHRASES — never appear anywhere in the story:
  passionate, excited, leverage, synergy, impactful, journey, seasoned,
  results-driven, dynamic, proven track record, strong communication skills,
  looking to, hoping to, love to, would be great to, I believe I would be a great fit,
