MICRO-SKILL: INFER WHY SUDHEER MADE THIS DECISION
You are given one decision Sudheer made: an approve, edit_approve, or skip.
You have the full context of what the agent showed him when he decided.
Your job: infer WHY he made this decision and extract a durable preference rule
that future agents can apply automatically.

ABOUT SUDHEER:
He has high standards. He is precise about who is worth contacting.
He skips recruiters always. He prefers 2nd degree connections.
He wants Engineering Managers and Senior ICs — not just any connection.
He edits messages that are too long, too generic, or contain filler language.
He approves messages that are specific, direct, and under 200 characters for senior people.

WHAT MAKES A GOOD INFERENCE:
Be specific. A vague inference helps no agent.

BAD inferred_why:
  "User preferred this contact over others"
  "User decided to skip this message"
  "User made edits to improve the message"

GOOD inferred_why:
  "Sudheer approved because the person is a 2nd degree Engineering Manager with
   AI signal in their headline — matches his strong preference for technical leads
   over non-technical managers at product companies"
  "Sudheer skipped despite a score of 7 because the title contained 'People' —
   consistent with his pattern of avoiding HR-adjacent titles regardless of seniority"
  "Sudheer edited to remove 'I hope this finds you well' and shorten from 240 to
   160 chars — consistent with his pattern of preferring ultra-short messages
   to Director-level contacts"

BAD inferred_preference:
  "User has preferences about contacts"
  "User prefers better messages"

GOOD inferred_preference:
  "Skip all profiles with People, Talent, HR, Recruiter, Sourcer, or Coordinator in title
   regardless of seniority or mutual connections"
  "For Director-level contacts, target messages under 170 characters — approved 5/5 in
   this range, edited 3/3 that exceeded 200 characters"
  "Lead with mutual connection hook when available — approved rate is 100% vs 71% for
   other hook types"

CONFIDENCE SCALE:
  5 — decision clearly fits a pattern seen multiple times before
  4 — strong signal, first or second time seeing it
  3 — moderate signal, could be situational
  2 — weak signal, hard to generalise from this single decision
  1 — no clear signal, log but do not apply as a rule

RETURN JSON with exactly these fields:
  inferred_why:        string — 1-2 sentences, specific, names the signals
  inferred_preference: string — one actionable rule agents can follow
  confidence:          integer 1-5
  category:            one of these exact values:
                       "CONTACT_TARGETING" — who to contact or skip
                       "MESSAGE_TONE"      — how to write or what phrases to use/avoid
                       "MESSAGE_STRUCTURE" — length, structure, hook type
                       "RELEVANCE_THRESHOLD" — score calibration
                       "COMPANY_CONTEXT"   — company type behaviour
